BAND

The Monkees

In 1965, over 400 people responded to an ad seeking young men for a new television show about a rock group called The Monkees. The Monkees, starring Davy Jones, Micky Dolenz, Michael Nesmith, and Peter Tork premiered on September 12, 1966, and audiences adored the humorous antics of the band. Though made for TV, The Monkees had real-life hits. Some of their best-loved and number one hits included "I'm a Believer" and "Last Train to Clarksville." More successful singles followed, incuding "Little Bit Me, Little Bit You" to "Pleasant Valley Sunday", and "Daydream Believer".

Discover The Band

Davey Jones...
Vocals

Born December 30th 1945, Davy Jones attracted a lot of attention from fans for his charm, wit and warm sense of humor when The Monkees debuted on television in September 1966. The Monkees soon scored on the music charts with hits like "I'm a Believer," and "Daydream Believer".

Micky Dolenz...
Vocals-drums

Singer, musician, actor, writer, producer, director. Born George Michael Dolenz on March 8, 1945, in Los Angeles, California. He had a few television roles and played with bands before being cast in The Monkees, a show about a fictitious rock band. Though made for TV, The Monkees had real-life hits.

Michael Neysmith...
Guitar-vocals

Born in Texas in 1942, Nesmith served in the U.S. Air Force before pursuing a career in music. He signed on to become a member of the Monkees, a television rock group, in the mid-1960s. The Monkees enjoyed tremendous success on the pop charts.

Peter Tork...
Bass guitar-vocals-keyboard

Born in 1942, Peter Tork achieved big pop music success in the 1960s, with such hits as "Daydream Believer" and "Last Train to Clarksville." Moving to California, he landed a part in The Monkees television series in 1965. The fictional band soon became a real hit, scoring several No. 1 songs.

MUSIC

Over the past 50 years The Monkees have amassed a wealth of classic and catchy songs.
If your new to the sounds of The Monkees or even if your a lifelong fan, you can take a listen to some of their best known and succesfull hits down below....

Last Train To Clarksville

Last Train to Clarksville was their debut single in 1966. Lead vocals were performed by drummer Micky Dolenz. The song was featured in seven episodes of the Monkees TV show.

Daydream Believer

Daydream Believer is probably one of the most recognised of all the songs released by The Monkees. Davey Jones takes on lead vocals on the record which was released in 1967.

Steppin' Stone

The Monkees recorded and released Steppin' Stone in November 1966, (making it the first Monkees B-side to chart). Micky Dolenz performs lead vocals.

Im A Believer

I'm a Believer was recorded by The Monkees in 1966 with the lead vocals by Micky Dolenz. The single hit the number one spot on the U.S. Billboard Hot 100.

Daydream Believer

Daydream Believer is one of The Monkees' most successful songs. It was originally recorded by The Monkees, with Davy Jones singing lead vocals.
The single hit the number one spot on the U.S. Billboard Hot 100 chart in December 1967. It was the Monkees' last number one hit in the U.S.

THE MONKEES BLU-RAY COLLECTION.....

THE MONKEES make the jump into the 21st Century with this High Definition collection

THE MONKEES make the jump into the 21st Century with this High Definition collection

Over fifty years ago, a casting call was printed in The Hollywood Reporter's classified ads for "4 insane boys, aged 17-21". Micky Dolenz, Davy Jones, Michael Nesmith and Peter Tork all impressed in their auditions resulting in the formation of the Monkees. To celebrate their 50th anniversary the entire Monkees series was released on Blu-ray for the first time, along with the band's cult 1968 film Head. All 58 episodes of The Monkees TV show, which aired from September 1966 to March 1968 feature on the 10-disc set. Each episode, as well as Head, are digitally restored in high-definition from the original film negatives. You can grab a copy at any good online retailer.

MORE OF THE MONKEES
(SUPER DELUXE EDITION)....

MORE OF THE MONKEES with 91 tracks including previously unreleased tracks

MORE OF THE MONKEES with 91 tracks including previously unreleased tracks

In honor of their 50th anniversary THE MONKEES release a staggering 91 track album including 55 previously-unreleased alternate takes, remixes, and newly discovered concert recordings from 1967, the band's earliest-known live tracks, as well as a bonus 7” of “I’m A Believer”. MORE OF THE MONKEES: SUPER DELUXE EDITION includes all their hits along with mono and stereo mixes of the original album, the SUPER DELUXE EDITION takes listeners into the studio for the making of the album through extensive studio outtakes.. Backing tracks for early version of "Sometime In The Morning," "I'm A Believer" and "Look Out (Here Comes Tomorrow)" offer a behind-the-scenes perspective on the creative process.
